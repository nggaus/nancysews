Syracuse Needs Locally Sewn Masks

Last update -- 01:09 PM, 3/26/20

Hello to my friends in central New York

As you have likely heard, there is a national shortage of surgical masks. Hospitals are in desperate need. They are beginning to take hand sewn masks as a stop gap until the shortages are alleviated.

There are plenty of patterns and advice online, and a lot of people jumping in to make masks, but it wasn’t clear to me that there was any organized effort for central New York (maybe, but I haven’t been able to find any). I wasn’t willing to proceed until I knew that 1) what I was making was acceptable; 2) I knew where they were needed locally; and 3) I knew how to deliver them without creating more confusion at busy hospitals and medical facilities.

I am finally getting answers. As of this writing, SUNY Upstate is beginning to take donations from people who are willing and able to make masks. There is a hospital committee that has looked into the materials and design needed to make them as safe as possible for their healthcare workers to use. I have posted their information below.

First, here is some basic information from the Open-Source COVID19 Situation and Supply Needs (OSCMS) group. They emphaize that you should be working with a group that is using a vetted design and has a distribution network setup so as not to overwhelm the hospitals. Don't reinvent the wheel and go it alone unless there is nothing set up in your area. I am slowly finding finding groups in central New York and as I find them, I will list them below. If you know of active groups that would like assistance, please let me know and I will add them along with their contact information to my list.

OSCMS provides a link to a document that enumerates a number of features and materials that are better for masks. This document also has a list of patterns for masks that are more likely to be approved by local hospitals. But again, you should be trying to make what the local hospitals and health centers actually need. If you have information on which center requires what, I can also add that to my list.

Questions that you probably have that I think I have answers for:

  • How do I know I have the right materials in my stash?
    Current instructions are calling for tightly woven, prewashed cotton (quilting cotton will do), flannel if you have it (for lining), narrow flat elastic or elastic cord, and some require a 5" nosepiece which is in limited supply locally (Note: You can substitute a heavy garden twist tie or pipe cleaner if necessary). If you don't have elastic, you may be able to replace it with narrow ties made from the cotton fabric.

  • I don’t have the materials at home. Where can I get them?
    Once you know what materials are acceptable (ask a working group or check approved instruction sheets), you can purchase locally from stores listed below.

  • I know how to sew but I don’t have a machine. Where can I get one?
    Ask friends and neighbors if they have any old machines in their closets. As long as it can sew a solid straight stitch, you’re good. If you can’t get your hands on one, then pair up with someone who does and offer to cut out fabric pieces or make a run to the fabric store for supplies (for front porch drop offs). Most fabric stores are now offering curb-side service.

    If you are really at a loss, you can sew the masks by hand. Make sure they are sturdy enough to stand up to repeated washing, though.

  • What design should I use and are there instructions?
    There are many designs out there. SUNY Upstate has just released their instruction sheet for a close fitting shaped mask. They will be distributing any extra of these sewn masks to other hospitals in Syracuse.

    Beth Eischen Call has these great instructions for the masks she is making for distribution to medical labs and other healthcare facilities.

    WeNeedMasks.org has information at the national level. Their designs may be suitable for healthcare facilities other than SUNY Upstate, but check first before you sew.
